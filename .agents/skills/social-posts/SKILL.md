## What to Focus On Instead

Posts should be **customer-centric** — useful, relevant, and focused on the customer's problem and how Wes solves it. Ask: *"Does this help the customer make a decision or understand their situation better?"*

Good angles:
- Practical tips the customer can act on ("here's how to check your boiler pressure")
- Real scenarios and outcomes ("no heating, two kids — sorted same day")
- Honest, specific advice ("when to repair vs replace")
- What makes the service work well for the customer (WhatsApp video saves time, part carries mean first-visit fixes)

Avoid:
- Listing credentials that don't mean anything to the customer in context
- Ending every post with the same boilerplate trust signals
- Padding posts with acronyms and cert numbers

## Platform Formatting Rules

### Facebook
- 80–200 words total
- Friendly, conversational tone — like talking to a neighbour
- 2–4 emojis (at line starts or end of post, not mid-sentence)
- Line breaks between points for readability
- End with the full service URL (must include `https://`, e.g. `https://bettercallwes.co.uk/services/boiler-repair.html`) on its own line, then: `Call/WhatsApp Wes: 07700 155 655`
- URLs MUST start with `https://` so they render as clickable links on Facebook
- Do NOT add hashtags to Facebook posts

### Instagram
- Hook line first (grabs attention in first 2 lines before "more...")
- 80–150 words body
- End with: `🔗 Link in bio`
- Blank line then 12–15 hashtags on separate lines
- Hashtag mix: local (`#Southampton #SO14 #SO15 #SO16 #SO17 #Eastleigh #Chandlerford`), trade (`#Plumber #GasSafe #GasSafeEngineer #HeatingEngineer`), topic (`#BoilerRepair #BoilerService #Heating`)
- Do NOT include the service URL in the Instagram body (links don't work in Instagram captions)

### Twitter/X
- Max 260 characters (strict — count them)
- Punchy, direct — no filler words
- 1–2 hashtags maximum
- Include the full service URL with `https://` (counts toward character limit, so keep text tight)
- Emojis optional but keep to 1–2

### Google Business (googlebusiness field)
- Similar to Facebook but keep to 100–150 words (Google Business posts are shorter)
- Professional, informative tone
- Include the full `https://` service URL
- No hashtags
- Optional: end with a clear CTA ("Book online:" or "Call/WhatsApp: 07700 155 655")
- Google Business posts appear in Google Search and Maps — great for local SEO

## Image Strategy

Each post needs an image. Choose the right type for the content:

| image_type | When to use | What Kie AI does |
|-----------|------------|-----------------|
| `brand` | Personal brand, trust signals | Takes a real photo of Wes and adds text caption overlay |
| `work` | Emergency, before/after, real jobs | Takes a real job photo and adds text caption overlay |
| `asset` | Tips, education, local | Takes a service graphic (boiler, radiator, van, etc.) and adds text caption overlay |
| `ai` | Pricing, conceptual, abstract | Generates a new image from scratch |

`caption`: The text that appears overlaid on the image. Keep it short — max 8 words, punchy, matches the post hook.

`image_prompt`:
- For `brand`/`work`/`asset`: Describe the text overlay treatment — e.g. "Add bold white text '[CAPTION]' as a clean caption banner at the top of this image. Semi-transparent dark background behind the text. Modern professional social media card style."
- For `ai`: Describe the full scene to generate — e.g. "Clean invoice on clipboard, 'Upfront pricing, no surprises' text visible, warm UK home setting, soft lighting, professional photography style."
